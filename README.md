## Project Overview

CloudTask Pro is a B2B SaaS Company that sells project management software. They are experiencing revenue growth yet facing customer churn. This project analyzes subscription, revenue, and customer behavior data to identify churn drivers, evaluate customer lifetime value, and provide recommendations for improving retention.

---

## Business Questions

1. How has churn changed over time?
2. Which plans and billing cycles have the highest churn?
3. What are the leading causes of churn?
4. Which plans generate the highest customer lifetime value?
5. What customer behaviors are associated with churn?

## Data Exploration  

### Revenue Dataset
* 48 monthly records
* Revenue, customer growth, churn, and acquisition metrics

### Subscription Dataset
* 600 customer records
* Subscription plan, billing cycle, customer characteristics, churn status, and engagement metrics
* 287 missing churn dates due to active customers

### Business Familiarization

The subscription dataset contains 600 customers across four subscription plans and two billing cycles.

**Plan Distribution**

* Starter: 217
* Professional: 173
* Business: 160
* Enterprise: 50

**Billing Cycle Distribution**

* Monthly: 352
* Annual: 248

**Customer Status**

* Churned: 313
* Active: 287

This distribution provides a balanced view of customer behavior across subscription tiers and billing cycles while highlighting the relatively small Enterprise customer segment.

### Key Metrics & Definitions

- **MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue):** Predictable subscription revenue generated each month
- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost):** Cost to acquire a new customer
- **CLV (Customer Lifetime Value):** Estimated revenue generated by a customer over their lifetime
- **NPS (Net Promoter Score):** Measures customer satisfaction and likelihood to recommend the product
- **Feature Usage %:** Percentage of platform features actively used by a customer
- **Support Tickets (12 Months):** Number of support requests submitted during the previous year

## Key Findings  

### Churn
* Monthly subscriptions churned at **60.51%** compared to **40.32%** for annual subscriptions
* Starter Monthly customers had the highest churn rate **(76.87%)**
* Enterprise customers had the lowest churn rate **(22%)**

### Churn Drivers
* Budget Cuts
* Price Too High
* Company Closed

### Customer Health
* Churned customers averaged 27.45% feature usage compared to 55.02% for active customers
* Churned customers averaged an NPS score of 3.04 compared to 5.81 for active customers

### Revenue
* Enterprise customers generated the highest average CLV **($66.6K)** and CLV:CAC ratio **(332.8)**
* Starter customers generated the lowest average CLV **($1.7K)** and CLV:CAC ratio **(8.4)**

---

## Recommendations  

* Encourage annual subscriptions through pricing incentives
* Improve onboarding and engagement for Starter customers
* Reassess pricing for small businesses
* Continue investing in Enterprise customer acquisition

## Limitations & Future Work

### Limitations
* The dataset did not include expansion MRR or churned MRR, preventing the calculation of true Net Revenue Retention (NRR)
* Churn risk analysis was limited to descriptive indicators rather than predictive modeling

### Future Work
* Build a churn prediction model using machine learning techniques
* Analyze customer retention by acquisition channel and industry
* Incorporate additional SaaS metrics such as Net Revenue Retention (NRR)
* Develop an interactive Tableau dashboard with filters and drill-down capabilities
